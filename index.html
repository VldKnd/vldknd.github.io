 Hello! I'm a software engineer with a strong interest in applied mathematics and Bayesian statistics. Over the course of my studies and professional experience, I've completed a variety of projects—ranging from simple front-end development to the design and implementation of complex machine learning systems. I'm currently based in France and always open to collaborations—feel free to reach out!

 I hold a Bachelor of Science in Applied Mathematics from Novosibirsk State University, where I specialized in statistical methods for clustering problems under the supervision of Vladimir Berikov. I later joined a dual-degree program involving Novosibirsk State University, Télécom Paris, and Institut Polytechnique, where I worked on various projects at the intersection of mathematics and machine learning.

 As part of my academic journey, I completed a research internship at the incredible Bell Labs under the supervision of Lou Salaün, where I proposed a novel heuristic for solving a restricted multi-agent pathfinding problem on graphs in polynomial time. After that, I transitioned into industry as an aspiring research engineer, contributing to drug discovery efforts at a Paris-based startup.

 I also collaborated with a research group at ENGIE Crigen Lab, exploring the application of generative modeling techniques for predicting molecular properties. Additionally, I completed a secondary program at the Yandex School of Data Analysis.
